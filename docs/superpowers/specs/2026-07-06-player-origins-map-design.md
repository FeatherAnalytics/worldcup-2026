# World Cup 2026 — Player Origins Map

## Overview

An interactive map visualization showing where 2026 FIFA World Cup players were born versus which national teams they represent. Three switchable views reveal different facets of the same dataset: talent diaspora flows, squad-level diversity, and country-level talent concentration.

Light, editorial aesthetic inspired by NYT interactives. Sits alongside urbanstack as a sibling portfolio project — shares typography DNA but establishes its own visual identity through a light basemap, warm color palette, and narrative-driven layout.

## Data Source

Static JSON files generated by the Python ETL pipeline:

- `data/output/players.json` — 1,248 player records with lat/lon birth coordinates, team, club, confederation, and `is_foreign_born` flag
- `data/output/summary.json` — pre-aggregated statistics for all four slicing dimensions (team, birth country, club, confederation)

No API required. Data loads at build time or on first client render.

## Views

### 1. Diaspora View (default)

**Purpose:** Show the global flow of talent — where players are born versus who they play for.

**Map layer:** deck.gl `ArcLayer` rendering great-circle arcs from each player's birth city (lat/lon) to the centroid of their team's country. Country centroids are pre-computed in `geo.ts` from the Natural Earth GeoJSON (polygon centroid calculation) — not hardcoded.

**Visual encoding:**
- Arc color: confederation of the team the player represents (see Color Palette)
- Arc width: uniform (1px base, 2px on hover)
- Arc height: proportional to distance (short arcs stay low, intercontinental arcs peak higher)
- Animation: arcs draw in sequentially on initial load (staggered by team)

**Interactions:**
- Hover an arc: tooltip with player name, birth city, team, club
- Filter panel: select by team, confederation, or birth country to isolate subsets
- "Show all" default; selecting a filter dims non-matching arcs to 10% opacity

**Key insight this view surfaces:** ~270 players (21.8%) represent a country different from where they were born. France alone has 98 players born on its soil spread across 13 different teams.

### 2. Squad Diversity View

**Purpose:** For a selected team, show where its 26 players were born on a world map.

**Map layer:** deck.gl `ScatterplotLayer` with dots at each player's birth coordinates.

**Visual encoding:**
- Dot color: foreign-born (warm accent) vs. home-born (neutral)
- Dot size: uniform (6px radius, 10px on hover)
- Team country highlighted with a subtle boundary outline

**Interactions:**
- Team selector: dropdown or clickable list in the side panel, sorted alphabetically
- Click a dot: expanded player card (name, position, club, birth city, caps/goals)
- Panel shows squad summary: total players, foreign-born count, birth countries represented
- Transition: dots animate in when switching teams (fade + scale)

**Key insight this view surfaces:** Morocco's squad has 18 of 26 players born abroad (mostly France and Spain). DR Congo has 20 of 26. Curaçao fields 24 foreign-born players.

### 3. Talent Factories View

**Purpose:** Which countries produce the most World Cup players, regardless of which team they play for?

**Map layer:** deck.gl `GeoJsonLayer` choropleth coloring countries by the number of World Cup players born there.

**Visual encoding:**
- Fill color: sequential warm scale (light cream → deep amber) based on player count
- Countries with 0 players: light gray (#f0f0f0)
- Country boundary stroke: thin white (0.5px)

**Data source for polygons:** Natural Earth 110m countries GeoJSON (public domain, ~800KB). Join on country name to `summary.json → birth_countries`.

**Interactions:**
- Hover a country: tooltip with player count, teams those players represent, top 3 players by caps
- Click a country: panel expands with full player list grouped by team they represent
- Legend: gradient bar with min/max labels

**Key insight this view surfaces:** France is the world's largest exporter of World Cup talent — 98 players born there represent 13 different nations. The Netherlands (66 players, 10 teams) and the United Kingdom (63 players, 10 teams) follow.

## Performance Considerations

- **players.json:** ~350KB — load once, filter client-side
- **countries.geojson:** ~800KB Natural Earth 110m — acceptable for initial load
- **Arc count:** up to 1,248 arcs in unfiltered diaspora view. deck.gl handles this easily (GPU-accelerated). No virtualization needed.
- **Static export:** zero server cost, CDN-friendly via GitHub Pages

## Accessibility

- Confederation colors tested for deuteranopia/protanopia distinguishability
- All interactive elements keyboard-accessible
- ARIA labels on map controls, view switcher, filters
- Tooltip content available via panel (not hover-only) for touch devices
- Choropleth legend with explicit numeric labels (not color-only)

## Out of Scope (v1)

- Dark mode toggle (light-only for editorial feel)
- Club-level analysis view (data exists but deferred to v2)
- Animated timeline / historical comparison
- Player search
- Embed/share functionality
- Server-side rendering or API backend
